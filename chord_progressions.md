# Chord Progressions by Emotional Feel

A comprehensive reference of chord progressions using Roman numeral notation.

**Convention:** Uppercase = Major, lowercase = minor, ° = diminished, + = augmented, 7 = dominant 7th, maj7 = major 7th, m7 = minor 7th, ø7 = half-diminished 7th, °7 = fully diminished 7th, sus = suspended

---

## Joyful / Uplifting / Triumphant

- **I – IV – V – I** — The foundational major cadence. Pure resolution and satisfaction.
- **I – V – vi – IV** — The most widely used pop progression. Bright with a touch of depth.
- **I – IV – I – V** — Simple, open, hymn-like joy.
- **I – ii – IV – V** — Warm buildup to a strong resolution.
- **I – IV – V – IV** — Rock anthem energy, driving and celebratory.
- **I – V – IV – V** — Forward-moving, confident optimism.
- **I – iii – IV – V** — Gentle lift with a brief minor color before resolving bright.
- **I – Imaj7 – IV – IV** — Lush, contented happiness with jazzy warmth.
- **I – IV – ii – V** — Classic and cheerful with smooth voice leading.
- **I – V/vi – vi – IV** — Anthemic with a secondary dominant adding intensity.
- **I – I+ – IV – iv** — Ascending chromatic line creates a swelling, cinematic joy.

---

## Sad / Melancholy / Sorrowful

- **vi – IV – I – V** — The "tragic" reframing of I–V–vi–IV. Starts from a place of loss.
- **i – iv – v – i** — Pure natural minor. Heavy, unresolved grief.
- **i – VI – III – VII** — Andalusian-adjacent. Drifting sadness without resolution.
- **i – iv – VI – V** — Minor with a brief major lift that makes the return to minor more painful.
- **i – V – i – iv** — The dominant V in minor creates desperate longing.
- **i – VII – VI – V** — Descending Andalusian cadence. Fatalistic, dramatic sorrow.
- **i – iv – VII – III** — Wandering minor progression with no firm resolution.
- **vi – V – IV – iii** — Descending stepwise motion in a major key. Resignation.
- **i – °7 – iv – V** — The diminished chord adds a stab of sharp anguish.
- **i – iv – i – V7** — Classical minor lament with dominant pull.
- **i – vm7 – VI – iv** — Deepened minor mood; the minor v avoids the dramatic dominant.
- **i – ivm7 – VImaj7 – Vm7** — Jazz ballad sadness. Smoky, introspective grief.

---

## Nostalgic / Bittersweet / Wistful

- **I – vi – IV – V** — The classic '50s progression. Warm memory and gentle longing.
- **Imaj7 – iii7 – IVmaj7 – iv** — Major to minor IV shift creates the ache of a fading memory.
- **I – iii – vi – IV** — Moves through gentle minor shading before returning to warmth.
- **I – V – vi – iii – IV – I – IV – V** — Pachelbel's Canon. Cyclical, timeless nostalgia.
- **I – vi – ii – V** — Jazz standard turnaround. Sophisticated, reminiscent warmth.
- **IVmaj7 – V7 – iii7 – vi** — The royal road progression (common in J-pop). Yearning beauty.
- **I – I7 – IV – iv** — The major-to-minor IV is the definitive bittersweet sound.
- **ii – V – I – vi** — Jazz turnaround starting from ii. Reflective, circular.
- **I – V – vi – IV – I – V – iii – IV** — Extended cycle that delays resolution. Lingering emotion.
- **I – III7 – vi – iv** — The secondary dominant to vi followed by minor iv creates a deep ache.

---

## Tense / Anxious / Uneasy

- **i – ii° – V – i** — The diminished ii° adds instability before the dominant.
- **i – °7 – i – °7** — Oscillation with diminished chords. Creeping dread.
- **V – iv – I (repeated)** — Backdoor approach with unresolved tension.
- **i – II – i – VII** — The major II (Neapolitan area) creates jarring displacement.
- **♭II – I – ♭II – I** — Phrygian oscillation. Unstable, confrontational.
- **i – ♭II – V – i** — Neapolitan sixth usage. Dark, operatic tension.
- **i – iv – ♭VII – ♭III – ♭VI – ♭II – V** — Circle of fifths in minor. Relentless harmonic pull.
- **i – v – ♭VI – iv** — Avoiding the dominant V keeps things murky and anxious.
- **i – ♭II7 – i – V7** — Tritone substitution proximity. Chromatic unease.
- **°7 – °7 – °7 (moving in minor 3rds)** — Symmetrical diminished movement. Vertigo and panic.

---

## Dark / Ominous / Sinister

- **i – ♭II – ♭VII – i** — Phrygian coloring. Ancient, foreboding darkness.
- **i – iv – ♭VI – ♭VII** — Power chord staple. Heavy, brooding metal/film darkness.
- **i – ♭VI – ♭III – ♭VII** — All natural minor chords. Cold, bleak landscape.
- **i – V+ – i – iv** — The augmented V is eerie and unsettling.
- **i – ♭VII – ♭VI – V** — Andalusian cadence. Dramatic, villainous resolve.
- **i – i – iv – iv – ♭VI – ♭VII – i – i** — Slow minor plateau. Cinematic, oppressive weight.
- **°7 – i (half-step above)** — Diminished approach. Jump-scare tension release.
- **i – ♭II – V – V+** — Neapolitan to augmented dominant. Distorted menace.
- **i – iv°7 – ♭VI+ – ♭II** — Layered altered chords. Nightmarish disorientation.
- **i – ♭vi – ♭III – ♭VII** — Lowered sixth degree adds occult coloring.

---

## Dreamy / Ethereal / Floating

- **Imaj7 – IVmaj7 – Imaj7 – IVmaj7** — Two-chord shimmer. Ambient, weightless.
- **Imaj7 – ♭VIImaj7 – IVmaj7 – Imaj7** — The ♭VII adds an unexpected lift, like breaking through clouds.
- **Imaj7 – iii7 – IVmaj7 – ♭VImaj7** — Chromatic median shift into a foreign key center. Surreal.
- **I – III – IV – iv** — Major III is borrowed and exotic; minor iv dissolves the brightness.
- **IVmaj7 – iii7 – ii7 – Imaj7** — Descending stepwise with 7ths. Drifting, slow-motion feel.
- **i – ♭III+ – ♭VI – iv** — Augmented chord as gateway to another tonal world.
- **Iadd9 – IVadd9 – vadd9 – Iadd9** — Added 9ths on everything. Shimmering, impressionistic.
- **Imaj7 – ♭IIImaj7 – ♭VImaj7 – IVmaj7** — Giant-steps-style major third movement. Kaleidoscopic.
- **ii7 – ♭II7 – Imaj7** — Tritone substitution resolution. Floating landing.
- **Imaj9 – ♭VImaj9 – IVmaj9 – ♭IImaj9** — Chromatic mediants with extended chords. Pure atmosphere.

## Romantic / Sensual / Intimate

- **Imaj7 – vi7 – ii7 – V7** — Jazz ballad standard. Warm, candlelit sophistication.
- **ii7 – V7 – Imaj7 – vi7** — Turnaround cycle. Swaying, dance-like romance.
- **Imaj7 – IV – iii7 – vi** — Gentle descent into the relative minor. Tender vulnerability.
- **I – V/vi – vi – IV – V** — The secondary dominant to vi intensifies emotional arrival.
- **IVmaj7 – iii7 – vi7 – ii7 – V7** — Extended chain of falling fifths. Lush and enveloping.
- **Imaj7 – ♭VImaj7 – Imaj7 – ♭VImaj7** — Chromatic mediant oscillation. Mysterious allure.
- **I – I+ – vi – IV** — The augmented I chord adds a sighing, reaching quality.
- **ii9 – V13 – Imaj9** — Extended jazz voicings. Velvet, late-night intimacy.
- **i – iv7 – ♭VImaj7 – V7** — Minor key romance. Passionate, almost painful beauty.
- **Imaj7 – II7 – IVmaj7 – ♭VII7** — Secondary dominants and borrowed chords. Smoldering heat.

## Heroic / Epic / Powerful

- **I – V – vi – iii – IV – I – IV – V** — Full Canon-style cycle. Grand, sweeping narrative.
- **I – ♭III – IV – I** — Borrowed ♭III adds a modal, mythic weight.
- **I – ♭VI – ♭VII – I** — Double plagal cadence. Cinematic, triumphant arrival.
- **i – ♭VII – ♭VI – ♭VII – i** — Aeolian vamp. Battle march, defiant strength.
- **I – V – ♭VII – IV** — Mixolydian borrowing. Rugged, adventurous heroism.
- **i – ♭III – ♭VII – IV** — Major IV in a minor context. Light breaking through darkness.
- **I – IV – ♭VI – ♭VII – I** — Double borrowed chords create a massive, cinematic lift.
- **I – ♭VII – IV – I** — Mixolydian rock cadence. Swagger and confidence.
- **vi – ♭VI – ♭VII – I** — Rising from minor depths to a major arrival. Rebirth moment.
- **i – iv – V – ♭VI – ♭VII – I** — Minor to parallel major key change. The hero's transformation.

---

## Mysterious / Suspenseful / Curious

- **i – ♭II – i – ♭II** — Phrygian oscillation. Sphinx-like, riddle energy.
- **Imaj7 – ♭IImaj7 – Imaj7 – ♭IImaj7** — Half-step shimmer in major. Uncanny elegance.
- **i – III+ – ♭VI – iv** — The augmented III pulls the ear toward unexpected territory.
- **i – ♭VII – ♭VI – ♭V(♯IV)** — Chromatic descent. Spiraling deeper into the unknown.
- **ii° – V – i – ♭VI** — Resolution undermined by the deceptive ♭VI. Twist ending.
- **i – v – ♭VI – IV** — Minor v keeps the dominant ambiguous. Fog and shadows.
- **ø7 – maj7 (half-step up)** — Half-diminished resolving up a half step. Puzzle-box harmony.
- **Isus4 – I – Isus2 – I** — Suspended chords orbiting the tonic. Something is about to happen.
- **i – ♭III+ – V – i** — Augmented mediant disrupts the path to the dominant.
- **Imaj7 – ♭VImaj7 – ♭IImaj7 – V7** — Giant chromatic drops. Noir detective atmosphere.

## Peaceful / Calm / Pastoral

- **I – IV – I – IV** — Two-chord major pendulum. Hymn-like serenity.
- **I – V – IV – I** — Plagal motion provides a gentle, "amen" quality.
- **Imaj7 – IVmaj7 – ii7 – V7** — Soft jazz voicings over a simple progression. Sunday morning.
- **I – iii – IV – I** — Brief minor color adds depth without disturbing the calm.
- **I – Iadd9 – IV – IVadd9** — Added 9ths create an open, pastoral ring.
- **I – vi – IV – I** — The vi adds a touch of warmth before the gentle plagal return.
- **Imaj7 – ii7 – iii7 – IVmaj7** — Stepwise ascending with 7ths. Gradual, sunrise feeling.
- **I – V – vi – IV (slow tempo)** — The ubiquitous four chords played slowly become meditative.
- **Isus2 – IV – V – Isus2** — Suspended tonics feel open, like a wide landscape.
- **I – IV – vi – V** — Gentle rearrangement. Soft resolution.

---

## Playful / Quirky / Whimsical

- **I – III – IV – iv** — The unexpected major III and minor iv create a wink.
- **I – ♯I° – ii – V** — Chromatic passing diminished. Tip-toeing, cartoonish.
- **I – II7 – IV – iv** — Secondary dominant to IV with a minor turn. Cheeky surprise.
- **I – ♭VII – IV – I** — Mixolydian shuffle. Carefree, tongue-in-cheek.
- **I – ♭III – IV – ♭VI** — All borrowed chords. Off-kilter, fairytale logic.
- **I+ – IV – ii° – V** — Augmented tonic and diminished ii. Stumbling and silly.
- **I – vi – ♯IV° – V** — The chromatic diminished is a brief hiccup of mischief.
- **Imaj7 – ♭IIImaj7 – Imaj7 – ♭IIImaj7** — Major third oscillation. Tilted, kaleidoscope whimsy.
- **I – V – ♭VII – IV – ♭VI – ♭III – ♭V(♯IV) – I** — Descending chromatic mediants. Wonderland spiral.

---

## Aggressive / Angry / Intense

- **i – ♭II – i – ♭II** — Phrygian hammer. Confrontational, rhythmic attack.
- **i – iv – i – V** — Bare minor with hard dominant pull. Clenched-fist energy.
- **I5 – ♭III5 – IV5 – I5** — Power chords (no thirds). Raw, distorted aggression.
- **i – ♭VII – ♭VI – V** — Andalusian with attitude. Driven, relentless fury.
- **i – ♭V(♯IV) – i – ♭V(♯IV)** — Tritone oscillation. Maximum dissonance and confrontation.
- **i – ♭II – ♭VII – i** — Phrygian dominant cluster. Thrash and fury.
- **i – iv – ♭II – V** — Neapolitan substitution mid-phrase. Sucker-punch harmonic shift.
- **I – ♭VII – ♭VI – ♭V** — Chromatic descent in power chords. Crushing, mechanical weight.
- **i – ii° – ♭II – V** — Diminished and Neapolitan back to back. Relentless dissonance.
- **°7 – °7 – i (all half-step approaches)** — Diminished stacking. Chaotic, explosive arrival.

## Hopeful / Inspirational / Upward

- **vi – VII – I** — Simple minor-to-major lift. Dawn breaking.
- **vi – IV – I – V** — Starts in shadow, resolves into light.
- **I – V – vi – IV – I – V – I** — The standard pop cycle that ends resolved. Journey completed.
- **i – ♭III – ♭VII – I** — Minor to parallel major. Clouds parting.
- **IV – V – vi – IV – V – I** — Deceptive cadence first, then true resolution. Earned hope.
- **ii – V – I – Imaj7** — Jazz resolution with the maj7 adding a sparkle of possibility.
- **i – iv – ♭VI – ♭VII – I** — Picardy-style resolution. Light at the end of the tunnel.
- **vi – V – IV – V – I** — Descending then rising. The valley before the summit.
- **I – IV – vi – V – I – IV – I** — Extended plagal ending. Benediction, blessing.
- **i – ♭VI – ♭III – ♭VII – I** — Long minor journey resolving to the parallel major. Redemption arc.

## Exotic / Modal / World-Influenced

- **i – ♭II – i – ♭II** — Phrygian. Spanish, Middle Eastern, Flamenco.
- **I – II – IV – I** — Lydian hint via major II chord. Bright, otherworldly.
- **i – IV – ♭VII – i** — Dorian mode (major IV in minor). Celtic, folk, medieval.
- **I – ♭VII – IV – I** — Mixolydian. Blues-rock, folk, Appalachian.
- **i – ♭II – V – i** — Phrygian dominant / Hijaz. Middle Eastern, Klezmer.
- **I – ♭II – ♭III – IV** — Ascending Phrygian in a major context. North African, Andalusian.
- **i – III – iv – ♭VII** — Mixed modal. Eastern European, Romani.
- **I – ♭VII – ♭III – IV** — Modal mixture. Celtic, Nordic.
- **i – ♭II – ♭III – iv – V** — Full Phrygian dominant scale harmonization. Flamenco.
- **I – II – ♭VII – I** — Lydian-Mixolydian blend. Indian raga-influenced, Gamelan.

## Funky / Groovy / Cool

- **i7 – IV7** — Two-chord minor funk vamp. Head-nodding pocket.
- **I7 – IV7 – I7 – V7** — Dominant 7ths throughout. Blues-funk swagger.
- **i9 – iv9** — Extended minor 9th vamp. Neo-soul smoothness.
- **I7 – ♭VII7 – IV7 – I7** — Mixolydian funk. Slippery, confident groove.
- **ii7 – V7 – ii7 – V7** — Unresolved turnaround. Perpetual motion funk-jazz.
- **I7 – II7 – IV7 – I7** — Chromatic dominant approach. James Brown territory.
- **i7 – ♭III7 – IV7 – i7** — Minor funk with major color. Parliament/Funkadelic energy.
- **I9 – ♭VII9 – I9 – ♭VII9** — Extended dominant oscillation. Modern R&B bounce.
- **i – iv – ♭VI7 – V7** — Minor groove with dominant tension. Motown-meets-hip-hop.
- **Im7 – IVm7 – ♭VImaj7 – V7sus4** — Suspended dominant landing. Smooth, unresolved cool.

## Spiritual / Transcendent / Sacred

- **I – IV – I – IV – V – I** — Hymn cadence. Simple, reverent devotion.
- **Imaj7 – IVmaj7 (sustained)** — Two-chord meditation. Mantra-like stillness.
- **i – ♭VII – ♭VI – ♭VII – i** — Aeolian chant. Monastic, ancient gravity.
- **I – ♭VI – ♭III – ♭VII – I** — Chromatic mediants resolving home. Cathedral-scale grandeur.
- **Isus2 – Isus4 – I** — Suspended resolution cycle. Bell-like, crystalline purity.
- **I – vi – IV – V – I (with plagal tag IV – I)** — Gospel close. Communal, affirming warmth.
- **IV – I – IV – I** — Plagal "amen" cadence. Ecclesiastical resolution.
- **i – ♭III – ♭VI – iv – V – I** — Minor pilgrimage to major resolution. Picardy transcendence.
- **I – iii – vi – ii – V – I** — Full diatonic circle. Ordered, cosmic harmony.
- **Imaj7 – ♭VImaj7 – IVmaj7 – Imaj7** — Chromatic mediant cycle. Otherworldly radiance.
